2 · What the research says

Method: 94 source-backed claims were extracted from current (2025–2026) studies and run through adversarial verification — 45 of 74 checks were refutes, which stripped out the vendor hype. Only claims that survived are used below.

The lever ranking (for a local, single-location business)

TierLeverWhy it winsLane
TIER 1 Google Business ProfileFoundational, heavily-weighted input; Google AI Mode surfaces GBP data even when it isn't the cited source. Basic AI presence is achievable from an optimized GBP alone.👤
Reviews (volume, recency, specificity, rating, owner responses)A primary trust filter. AI-recommended businesses averaged ~4.1–4.3★. Reviews naming the service + location carry more weight than generic ones.👤

Use 40–60 word answer blocks under question-style headings ("What is…?", "Who is it for?"); one page per service. Keep copy as real HTML text — not trapped in images/sliders — so AI crawlers can read it.✍️ 🤖

TIER 3 Local "best of" listicles + light local PRMildly helpful (getting into a "top medspas in Katy" article). The dramatic "third-party drives 85%" stat is B2B-SaaS, not local — local citations are ~86% brand-controlled.👤
Blog / educational postsLow-leverage for a local spa. Only pays off if answer-first, tightly structured, locally focused, entity-linked, and kept fresh — and still ranks behind everything above.🤖
The key strategic split — objective vs subjective queries:
• Subjective ("best facial spa in Katy") → driven by reviews + Yelp + third-party roundups + GBP. Your website barely moves these. → 👤 Tier 1
• Objective / branded ("does GV do microneedling," hours, a specific treatment) → driven by your own site + GBP as the "truth anchor." → ✍️ 🤖 Tier 2
GV's tracking gaps are mostly subjective discovery (q1–q8) and problem-aware (q17–q24) queries — so Tier 1 owner levers are what will actually move them.

Also true: ChatGPT aligns ~87% with Bing's top results, and Google AI Overviews pull from the organic index — so classic SEO + map-pack ranking still feed AEO. It's an overlapping game, not a separate one.

Discarded as hype (refuted): "schema = 92% lift," "GBP weighted 35% / reviews 2.8–3.2×" (single vendor, no methodology), "listicles drive 90% / third-party 6.5×" (B2B-SaaS scope), "AI recommends only 1.2% of businesses" (multi-location enterprise scope, not single-location spas).

3 · The plan — by leverage

A · Google Business Profile  👤 OWNER · highest leverage

  • Complete every field: categories (primary + secondary), full granular services list, attributes (incl. languages spoken — Spanish), hours, service area (Katy, Cinco Ranch, Fulshear, west Houston).
  • Add 80+ current photos; post weekly (offers, treatments, before/afters).
  • Fill the Q&A section with the real questions customers ask.
  • Keep it active and accurate — staleness gets you deprioritized.

I can't edit GBP (it's Google, not Wix), but I can draft your categories, services list, Q&A, and post copy.

B · Review engine  👤 OWNER

  • Systematize asking every happy client for a Google review (text/QR at checkout, follow-up).
  • Prioritize recency (a steady trickle beats a one-time burst) and specificity (encourage mentioning the treatment + "Katy").
  • Respond to every review, good and bad — engagement is itself a signal.
  • Mirror onto Yelp + Facebook so the off-site review picture is consistent.

C · NAP / listings consistency  👤 OWNER ✍️ I audit

  • Pick one canonical name ("GV Skincare Center") and make name/address/phone/hours identical everywhere — the site varies between GV SKINCARE / GV Skincare Center / GV Skin Care Center today.
  • Claim/correct Yelp, Apple Maps, Facebook, Foursquare, Bing Places, and major local directories.
  • I'll produce the exact canonical NAP block + a checklist of listings to fix.

D · On-site service pages + FAQ + schema  ✍️ EDITOR 🤖 API

  • One dedicated, answer-first page per core service (HydraFacial, microneedling, RF microneedling, chemical peel, acne facial, body contouring, lymphatic drainage) — direct answer first, then detail, with "who it's for / what to expect / cost."
  • Add a 5–7 question FAQ block to the homepage and each service page; lead each answer with one quotable sentence.
  • Rewrite inner-page titles/meta to include service + "Katy, TX"; fix the /copy-of-virtual-consultation slug + 301; add an English virtual-consultation page.
  • Lean into bilingual: the Spanish-speaking esthetician angle is a genuine local differentiator — surface Spanish service content and answer Spanish-language queries (mirrors tracked q37–q40).
  • Add Service, FAQPage, Person (Gabriela) and Review schema. (Static-page copy + schema = paste-ready drafts I give you; robots.txt and any content-item SEO I do via API.)

E · Local "best of" inclusion + light PR  👤 OWNER · modest

  • Get listed in "best medspas / facials in Katy" roundups and local press; aim to appear in the first few names (position in a listicle matters).
  • Partner with nearby complementary businesses (bridal, gyms, wellness) for mentions/links.

F · Blog  🤖 API · optional, conditional

Only if Tiers A–D are underway and you want incremental reach on problem-aware queries. If we do it, posts must be answer-first, locally framed, entity-linked, and refreshed regularly — and tied to specific tracked queries (acne scars, melasma, teen acne). Skipping or deferring this is a reasonable choice; it is not where the wins are.

5 · How we'll measure

The tracking harness re-runs the 40-query set across the AI engines and feeds the AEO Dashboard. Expect the subjective discovery/problem queries (q1–q8, q17–q24) to respond to the Tier 1 owner levers (GBP + reviews + listings), and objective/branded queries to respond to the Tier 2 site work. We re-run after each phase and watch:

  • Visibility score — % of non-branded queries where GV appears, per engine.
  • Citation share — % of organic citations pointing to gvskincare.com.
  • Query coverage — which categories flip from absent → present.
Net: invest first where the evidence points — Google Business Profile, reviews, and listing consistency — then answer-first service pages + FAQs on the site. The blog is optional and last.
